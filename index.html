Five Stages of Grief Feed

Denial 7/99
Denial is the beginning of the grief process typically resulting in the individual becoming numb to the reality of the loss they have experienced. #MentalHealthAwareness
Anger 8/99
Anger is the next step and unsurprisingly this emotion to be directed toward the persons own self but can also lead to feelings of anger towards people and objects within their lives. #MentalHealthAwareness
Bargaining 9/99
When faced with the overwhelming reality of potentially losing a loved one many may begin to bargain with a sense of existentialism so as to avoid the loss and pain that will inevitably come about from it. #MentalHealthAwareness
Depression 10/99
One of the worst aspects of grief is the onset of depression as the reality of the situation becomes present in the individuals life and many negative thoughts can come about from it, including suicidal thoughts. #MentalHealthAwareness
Acceptance 11/99
As an indivdual moves past the depressive stage and truly comes to terms with the loss they have endured they can begin to move forward with their life while also readjusting the way they operate to fill the gaps created by said loss.
